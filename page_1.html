What is that number?

In 2015, Marysville produced 22,500 tons of waste. Where does this waste go? A lot of them go to landfills and that can be detrimental to the carbon footprint. You may be asking how so? To answer this question, we need to look at the pollutants in our trash, Methane. It is released through the organic matter in landfills. And it accounts for approximately 15.1% of greenhouse gas emissions.








What is Recycling?

Recycling is the process of taking something used and reusing the materials for something else. It helps because we have a limited amount of resources on our planet and it can help reduce landfills.

Recycling in Marysville

In Marysville, recycling trucks come and pick up our recyclable materials. Those materials go to get filtered and some will go to factories which will turn those materials into usable products for something else.

What to Recycle

Rigid Plastics/Bottles, Paper and Cardboard, Metals, Glass, Loose Plastic BagsPolystyrene Foam Cups or Containers and Soiled Food Items can be recycled. Clothes, bricks and plasters can be recycled as well.

Don't have easy access to recycling?

If you don't have curbside recycling, you can separate your trash and manually send the recyclables to a recycling drop-off center. The nearest centers for Marysville are Moots recycling and Union recycling
